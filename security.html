Security Engineer

Security Engineers generally have more independent jobs, as they can be hired on a situational basis or just work for a company. A subset of security engineers is known as a penetration tester or a white hat hacker, and theyâ€™re main job is to test the security of a company, often focusing on the technology aspect of it. This means that either the CEO, or board of the company will hire (a) security engineer(s), and not tell the rest of the employees. The hacker must then attempt to exploit the security of the company, by using software methods or even by social engineering or breaking in. They do this with the intent of finding security flaws that the company can then fix, avoiding an actual attacker using the same exploit. While there is a wide range of possible skills depending on what specifically you want to do, good languages to learn for this job are Python, Ruby, C, and C++.
